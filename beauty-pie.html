The challenge

Customers were comparing off-site

Customers were already comparing Beauty Pie products to premium brands as part of their purchase journey — but this behaviour was happening off-site. Research showed strong demand for comparison-led content, particularly among younger audiences, and over half of interviewed users referenced comparison as influencing their decisions.


However, the website did not meaningfully support this behaviour, creating friction and reducing confidence at the point of purchase. The work needed to be designed and delivered within a tight two-week sprint.

The solution

A transparent comparison framework

I designed a structured comparison framework embedded across PLP, PDP, and dedicated comparison views. This included clear side-by-side tables highlighting price, performance, key ingredients, and service benefits — alongside simplified ingredient summaries to make complex formulations easy to understand.

01
Dedicated Compare PLP
A dedicated product listing page with direct compare access to the Beauty Pie "Compare" PLP. The layout stands out from other Beauty Pie compare pages, using a scannability-first approach with clear visual hierarchy.
02
PDP Compare Product Flyout
Users can compare directly from the PDP in a dedicated flyout panel. The flyout shows clear comparison information to the customer, with the membership toggle allowing real-time price comparison between member and non-member pricing.
03
PDP Anchor to Compare Table
The PDP surfaces an anchor link that allows users to jump directly to the comparison table without leaving the page — reducing friction and keeping the user in the purchase journey at the critical decision moment.
Results

Impact delivered

The comparison framework increased engagement with product pages, reduced reliance on external research, and improved customer confidence during decision-making. By reframing comparison as a transparent value narrative, the work strengthened trust in the brand and established a scalable model for future product categories.

📈
Increased engagement
Higher engagement with product pages as users spent more time exploring comparison data on-site rather than leaving to research elsewhere.
🎯
Reduced off-site research

🏗️
Scalable model
